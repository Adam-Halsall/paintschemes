Death Guard

The Death Guard paint scheme is based on a mix of a couple of inspirations from the codex and other images.

The vectorum inspiration is a cross between The Pallid Hand and Apostles of Contagion.

The goal is to create dirty cream armour with heavy use of bronze metallics, particularly on the shoulder guards. The small armour plates should be trimmed with a brighter green.

Additionally, this is a really good guide: https://brushandboltgun.com/2017/10/15/death-guard-painting-techniques/

Plague Marines

Undercoat

  1. Skull white spray

Cream Armour

  1. Screaming Skull on all armour
  2. Wash heavily with Athonian Camoshade
  3. Clean up armour plate surfaces with Screaming Skull, leaving the recesses and holes/pits gross and green
  4. The rims of armour plates are painted Elysian Green
  5. Add some drips and spills of Athonian Camoshade to holes and cracks in the armour

Flesh Pipes

  1. Paint the metal pieces of pipe inside the flesh Leadbelcher
  2. Clean up any mistakes on the flesh pipes with white
  3. Wash flesh pipes with Reikland Fleshshade, add some Athonian Camoshade in patches if wanting more gross
  4. This is a guideline, there is no strict rule for flesh pipes other than being gross

Horns

  1. Rakarth Flesh
  2. Seraphim Sepia wash
  3. Leaving the base of the horn dark with wash, paint Ushabti Bone over the rest of the horn
  4. Drybrush Mournfang Brown down a third of the way from the top
  5. Drybrush Abaddon Black halfway down the Mournfang Brown from the top

Metal

  1. Leadbelcher
  2. Nuln Oil wash
  3. Potentially use patches of Typhus Corrosion technical paint on things like chainmail

Bronze

  1. Balthasar Gold
  2. Agrax Earthshade wash
  3. Highlight raised areas with Balthasar Gold

Green Shoulders

  1. Castellan Green

Fleshy Poxwalkers

Undercoat

  1. Skull white spray

Everything Else

  1. Two wash layers of Reikland Fleshshade will achieve a caucasian fleshy shade
    • Two wash layers of anything will achieve a decent flesh i.e. a red wash then Reikland Fleshshade will make someone look boiled.
  2. Paint boils a gross green or yellow
  3. Tentacles washed Druchii Violet
  4. All other detail is adhoc/whatever

Green Poxwalkers

  1. Wraithbone spray
  2. One layer of Plaguebearer flesh for bright green
  3. Black Templar contrast paint on horns
  4. Paint all boils Wraithbone again

Base

  1. Paint the entire base with Stirland Battlemire, leaving some shallower areas
  2. Drybrush the bash lightly with Averland Sunset
  3. Fill the shallows with Nurgleâ€™s Rot technical paint
  4. Paint the rim black

Nurgle Daemons

Nurgle Daemons are a quick paint scheme intended to be speedy but good.

Nurgle Default

  1. Wraithbone spray
  2. Two layers of Plaguebearer Flesh contrast paint for a deep gross green
  3. Clean up anything not flesh with Wraithbone
  4. Flesh Tearers Red contrast on exposed/ripped flesh
  5. Skeleton Horde contrast paint on bones and horns
  6. Black Templar contrast paint on plagueswords
  7. Same bronze technique as Death Guard but use some Nihilakh Oxide afterwards

Great Unclean One

  1. Same as Nurgle Default but took some cues from the WarhammerTV GUO painting guide for stuff like stone and toe/fingernails.

Base

  1. Based the exact same way as Death Guard.

Genestealer Cult

The entire paint scheme is taken from the Genestealer Cult painting guide for the Blessed Wormlings cult.

The way to batch paint these is easier when blocking out whole colours for every layer so we're gonna do this scheme structured differently.

Undercoat

  1. Mechanicus Standard Grey spray

Standard Troop Armour

Basecoat

  1. Armour: Zandri Dust
  2. Rubber Armour: Incubi Darkness
  3. Red Cloth: Khorne Red
  4. Metal: Leadbelcher
  5. Dark Brown Leather (like gloves): Rhinox Hide
  6. Light Brown Leather: Mournfang Brown
  7. Brass: Balthasar Gold

Shade

  1. Armour: Reikland Fleshshade
  2. Rubber, Dark Brown Leather, Brass, Red Cloth: Nuln Oil
  3. Fatigues, Light Brown Leather, Metal: Agrax Earthshade

Layer 1

  1. Armour: Ushabti Bone - leave the recesses a bit darker
  2. Rubber: Kabalite Green - use the side of your brush to lightly paint it over the rubber ridges while leaving the recesses
  3. Red Cloth: Wazdakka Red
  4. Fatigues: Dawnstone
  5. Dark Brown Leather: Gorthor Brown - just edge highlight with it
  6. Light Brown Leather: Skrag Brown - just do raised areas

Layer 2

  1. Armour: Pallid Wych Flesh - just around the rim of the neck hole for the armour
  2. Rubber: Sybarite Green - same technique as the last rubber layer but lighter
  3. Red Cloth: Wild Rider Red - edge highlight
  4. Metal: Runefang Steel - edge highlight

Genestealers

Carapace

  1. Basecoat: Kantor Blue
  2. Shade: Nuln Oil
  3. Reapply Kantor Blue on raised areas, leaving darkened recesses
  4. Paint the segment edges with Alaitoc Blue
  5. Paint finer edge lines with Lothern Blue

Flesh

  1. Warpfiend Grey
  2. Wash with Druchii Violet
  3. Repaint raised areas with Warpfiend Grey
  4. Edge highlight raised areas with Slaanesh Grey

Base

  1. If based white, slap down some Rhinox Hide to brown it up
  2. Partial or full coverage of Stirland Battlemire
  3. If partially muddied, add some dark green static grass/tufts

Tyranids

This scheme is basically a rip-off of https://www.dakkadakka.com/dakkaforum/posts/list/462698.page but with some tweaks.

The colour scheme is intended to be very quick to paint with a good effect. Seeing a pattern here?

Undercoat

  1. Skull white spray

Skin

  1. Two coats of Biel-Tan Green wash
  2. Ushabti Bone layer or two leaving recess showing (thinned for areas like wing membranes)

Fleshy Bits

  1. As skin but the second wash layer is Druchii Violet
  2. Light drybrush of a bit of Ushabti Bone

Carapace

  1. Thick coat of Dark Angels Green contrast
  2. Heavy drybrush Kabalite Green
  3. Lighter drybrush of Warboss Green
  4. Even lighter drybrush of Moot Green

Claws/Weapons

  1. Stegadon Scale Green
  2. Highlight Russ Grey

All the other bits

  1. Eyes: Averland Sunset
  2. Teeth: Pallid Wych Flesh
  3. Tongue/Fleshy Bits: Bugman's Glow

Base

  1. Tyranids are based the exact same as Genestealer Cult

Snakebite Orks

Snakebites are a lot more free form than other armies. Intended to be fast as possible (cause mass boyz) but still look good.

Undercoat

  1. SOME OF THAT WRAITHBONE, WE ALL ABOUT THAT LIFE

Ork Skin

Do Ork Skin first because it's messy.

  1. Slap down Ork Flesh all over the the skin
  2. Drybrush all the skin with Elysian Green

Leathers/Cloth

  1. Leather is always Snakebite Leather

This is where we start getting a bit more free form. Use a random assortment of the following colours for cloth:

  1. Gore-Grunta Fur
  2. Wyldwood
  3. Black Templar
  4. Basilicanum Grey

Every now and then do a splash of colour on cloth with the following, stopping on the level of brightness looks best:

  1. Stegadon Scale Green
  2. Wash with Coelia Greenshade
  3. Sotek Green
  4. Temple Guard Blue

Armour

Armour for Snakebites is a bit free form too. Influence from multiple WarhammerTV videos. Base with:

  1. Steel Legion Drab - a favourite
  2. Mournfang Brown
  3. Doombull Brown
  1. Wash with Reikland Fleshshade

Then highlight with another brown or drybrush with Leadbelcher depending on the look you want

Bone

  1. Rakarth Flesh
  2. Seraphim Sepia
  3. Ushabti Bone on raised areas, leaving dark recesses

Squigs

Haven't painted many Squigs at this stage, subject to a lot of change.

  1. Flesh Tearers Red skin
  2. Black Templars hair

Base

  1. Agrellan Badland texture paint
  2. Wash entire base with Casandora Yellow
  3. Drybrush with Karak Stone
  4. Add dried yellow grass tufts

Black Dragons

When I first started Black Dragons, it involved a black undercoat and heaps of edge highlighting. It was so tedious in turned me off the entire project until I discovered the current scheme in an instagram post.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Joe (A) (@armageddon_bound) on Nov 5, 2019 at 9:57pm PST

The content of the post will be copied here vebatim in case the post ever disappears:

Undercoat

  1. Black undercoat spray

Entire Mini

  1. Drybrush entire mini liberally with Eshin Grey
  2. Drbyrush entire mini much lighter with Dawnstone
  3. Paint all the metal bits Leadbelcher
  4. Paint all the white bits Celestra Grey
  5. Leather with Rhinox Hide
  6. Wash entire mini with Nuln Oil
  7. Layer white bits with Ulthuan Grey, leaving dark recesses
  8. Layer leather with Gorthor Brown
  9. Gold parts with Retributor Gold
  10. Wash gold with Reikland Fleshshade
  11. Edge hightlight metal with Runefang Steel
  12. Paint all the skin, purity seals

Base

  1. Astrogranite
  2. Wash entire base with Agrax Earthshade
  3. Drybrush entire base very lightly with Karak Stone
  4. Patches of Valhallan Blizzard snow technical paint

Dark Eldar

The Dark Eldar schemes are mostly the default schemes (cause I was more excited about the army than the scheme when I started them) with a few tweaks.

Kabalite Warriors

Dark Eldar Pale Flesh

  1. Ionrach Skin
  2. Wash with Druchii Violet
  3. Layer nicely with Deepkin Flesh

Undercoat

  1. Black spray

Armour

  1. Incubi Darkness
  2. Wash with Nuln Oil but try Coelia Greenshade soon
  3. Relatively thick edge highlight armour plates with Kabalite Green
  4. Fine edge highlight armour plates with Sybarite Green

Metal

  1. Leadbelcher
  2. Wash with Nuln Oil
  3. Potentially layer with Ironbreaker

Brass

  1. Balthasar Gold
  2. Agrax Earthshade
  3. Gehenna's Gold

House Degallio

Supposed to allow a fast/good paintjob for an Imperial Guard force while still looking nice on a Knight. Still need to figure out how the yellow will work in this scheme.

Knights

Undercoat

  1. Wraithbone spray for panels
  2. Leadbelcher spray for metal

Degallio Guard

Undercoat

  1. Wraithbone spray

Infantry

  1. Basilicanum Grey for all the fatigues
  2. Snakebite Leather for the leather
  3. Leadbelcher for the metal
  4. Wash entire mini with Agrax Earthshade
  5. Paint Wraithbone over the armour panels again, leaving the recesses
  6. Runefang Steel highlights on the metal

Base

  1. Paint the base a solid colour to cover splotches.
  2. Cover entire base in PVA glue.
  3. Dip in Geek Gaming Scenic's Mediterranean Soil
